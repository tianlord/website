    • New Riders

      First time riders? This is what you need to know...

      Safe operation...

      Scooters are limited speed motorcycles. They are automatic (and do not require changing gears), but they do require skill and practice to operate safely. All riders must be 19+ and have a (non-L) licence to rent from us. Another prerequisite is that riders must be very competent and comfortable on a bicycle. Familiarity with being on 2-wheels is essential before taking the next step up to a motorcycle/scooter. Our (49cc) scooters do not require a motorcycle licence, but in Canada, anything more powerful does.

       

      Safe operation requires:

      • excellent balance
      • use of a throttle to control speed
      • an understanding of the bike's controls and signals
      • experience driving in traffic
      • good judgment skills    

      Can anyone ride a scooter?

      Scooter riding is a lot of fun, but it is not for everyone. We do include an orientation to the scooter, a demonstration and step-by-step instruction for new riders, and allocate time before each rental in our practice lot. We do our best to teach best riding practices in a fun and encouraging environment, however, from time to time we meet riders who are simply not quite ready for the road. For the safety of these riders, and others on island, we retain the right to refuse rental to those who cannot show proficiency in our practice lot.

       

      Proficiency means that a rider:

      • is confident and able to maintain a safe and controlled speed
      • is able to stop safely 
      • understands how to operate the scooter on different surfaces and in different kids of weather
      • understands how to to operate lights and controls
      • is able to demonstrate a controlled turn
      • understands traffic rules and regulations
      Our scooters are very user friendly, and we can teach most people how to ride, but it is important to understand the skills required to do so safely. Our first priority is ensuring the safety of all of our riders!

      The next step?

      If you are interested in taking a scooter riding course either before renting a Zoom Zoom scooter, or, if you would like to improve your skills to ride a more powerful scooter / motorcycle after learning with us, we highly recommend ProRide for professional and quality instruction. ProRide offers courses for all levels and interests, including certification through ICBC. We also offer a 10% discount to all ProRide graduates who ride with us at Zoom Zoom - just bring a copy of your course completion certificate or your skills test.

    • Getting Here

      Bowen Island is easily accessed from Horseshoe Bay Ferry Terminal in West Vancouver, BC. The 20-minute crossing is breathtaking and an adventure in itself. Keep your eyes peeled for sea lions, whales and eagles as you make the journey across the water flanked by our majestic coastal mountains. For more information on ferry schedules and fares, please visit the BC Ferries website for detailed and up to date information on the Horseshoe Bay to Snug Cove route.

      You can leave your troubles (and your car) behind. We've got your transportation covered. It is only a 5-minute walk from the ferry terminal to our location, straight up from the ferry and info centre at the corner of Miller Road and Trunk Road.

    • Our Commitment to the Environment

      We are a "Carbon Positive" company. That means that by riding around Bowen Island with Zoom Zoom,

      you are doing something GOOD for the planet.

      It is Bowen's natural splendour which brings so many people here, and we value the importance of protecting the environment - both locally and globally.

       

      That is why Zoom Zoom Bowen has taken the steps to become a “carbon positive" company by committing to purchasing a total of five tonnes of carbon offsets - that is FIVE TIMES our annual emissions. Through a partnership with the Vancouver-based company Offsetters, Zoom Zoom is now in its second year of supporting the Quadra Island Forestland Conservation Project, which acts as a carbon sink and protects biodiversity on the island, making Zoom Zoom, a "carbon positive" company.

       

      It is important to us at Zoom Zoom that we operate as ethically as possible, including doing our best to address our carbon footprint. and Our scooters are well maintained and have very low emissions to begin with, their availability for rent on island means that more people can now leave their cars at home. Ultimately, we wanted to take it one step further by committing to remaining "carbon positive" and doing our part to effect change.

    • Rental Requirements, Terms & Conditions

      The fine print.

      Rental Requirements & Licensing

      Please note the following requirements:

      1. A valid driver's license is required for all scooter rentals. Renters with a learner's license (L) are not permitted to rent a scooter. Renter must have the licence WITH them in order to rent. There will be no exceptions. 
      2. Any driver of a rented scooter must be19 years of age or older.
      3. A valid International Driver's Permit is required for licenses that do not meet Canadian standards.
      4. The renter must be qualified and possess the skills and experience necessary to safely operate the scooter they rent. The renter must be able to demonstrate safe operation of the rental scooter.
      5. A damage deposit of $300.00 per scooter is required for all scooter rentals. As long as the scooter is returned  in the same condition that it was rented, the deductible will be refunded. Renters are responsible for the cost of damage to scooters, and this cost will be taken from the deposit.

      Our Terms and Conditions

      All Renters are subject to the following policies:

      ​General:

      1. Renters must read, understand and sign the Rental Agreement and Liability Release Form provided at the time of rental. 
      2. All scooters must remain on Bowen Island at all times. 
      3. Only the renter who has signed the rental agreement and liability release is permitted to operate, or ride on, the rented scooter.
      4. A DOT helmet will be provided and must be worn at all times during operation of the scooter. 
      5. All renters are subject to BC Traffic Law and must follow the rules of the road accordingly. Renters may NOT take the scooters off-roading on trails, and must remain on public roads at all times.
      6. Renters must be present and in the company of a Zoom Zoom staff member for both the initial rental of the scooter as well as final inspection and return of the scooter. 
      7. Alcohol may not be consumed while operating the vehicle. 
      8. Smoking is not permitted while operating the vehicle. 

      Breakdowns, Flat Tires and Accidents:

       

      Our scooters are maintained regularly and we strive to keep them in excellent condition. We ask that renters return the scooters in the same condition they were when rented out.

      1. Zoom Zoom Bowen will provide the renter with contact numbers for support in the case of an emergency or a breakdown.
      2. In the case of an accident involving injury, the renter should always contact emergency services before contacting Zoom Zoom for support.
      3. In the event that a mechanical problem should occur, the renter is responsible for reporting it to Zoom Zoom Bowen as soon as possible. We do carry “back up” scooters and will do what we can to get it to you should the bike need to be taken off the road for repairs.
      4. In the case of a flat tire, please contact your Zoom Zoom Bowen staff member for help. The renter will be charged $25.00 to partially cover the cost the repair. All replacement tires must match the vehicle's manufacture's specifications, and must meet the Transportation Safety Board of Canada's standards.
      5. In the case of an accident that relates in damage to the scooter, the renter will be responsible for the cost of the repair up to the maximum deposit amount of $300. Scooter body damage such as scrapes and dents will be deducted from the $300 deductible. We do not allow renters to arrange repairs or provide replacement parts independently as we must ensure that they meet industry standards.

      Cancellations and Refunds:

      1. Scooters may be reserved by paying 25% of the full rental fee.
      2. The balance of the rental fee, along with the pre-authorized damage deposit of $300, will be collected at the time of the rental.
      3. No refunds or credits will be issued for scooters returned before the arranged return time.
      4. Cancelations as a result of the renter's failure to meet the rental requirements and licensing ((noted above) are the renter's responsibility. Cancellations are subject to the full value of the rental with no credit or refund to be issued.
